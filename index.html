Marcus Aurelius

The Philosopher Emperor or the Emperor Philosopher

The great Marcus Aurelius is well known for many things but the most important things would be his management of a great empire and his work in philosophy. He was respected in his time and his will to truly lead the empire in the right path was recognized by the politicians and the normal people.

Marcus Aurelius is said to have been the last good Emperor of Rome for multiple reasons, but one of them is that after Marcus died, the territory of Rome started to become smaller and smaller. Corruption flew in the veins of senators and emperors and the empire was slowly brought to its knees.

Here's a brief chronology of Marcus Aurelius' life:

  • 26. April, 121. - Born in Rome
  • 124. - Marcus' father dies, his grandfather adopts him
  • 138. - Marcus' uncle also adopts him too and inherits the throne from Hadrian
  • 138. - Gets bethroted to Faustine
  • 140. - Becomes a councilor
  • 145. - Gets married
  • 147. - He has his first child
  • 155. - 161. - Marcus' mother dies
  • 161. - Marcus and Lucius inherit the throne
  • 161. - Has two twin boys, one of them is Komodo
  • 166. - 167. - Plague in Rome
  • 168. - 180. - Wars on the northern front
  • 169. - Lucius dies
  • 175. - Revolt of Syria
  • 175. - Faustine dies
  • 175. - Komodo, his only alive son becomes emperor alongside Marcus
  • 17. March, 180. - Marcus Aurelius dies
